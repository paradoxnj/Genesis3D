Camera Mode

Camera Mode is used to navigate through the geometry of the level. This mode provides very fine control over the positioning of the camera in each of the four views.

For the three orthographic views (Top, Front and Side), the camera controls are very simple. You can pan the view (move from side to side and up/down) by holding the left mouse button and moving the mouse. To zoom in or out, hold the right mouse button and move the mouse up or down. You can also zoom by pressing the keypad '+' and '-' keys. Note that you don't have to be in camera mode in order for the '+', and '-' keys to work.

When zooming, the fine and coarse grids may change precision to reflect the new view point. The current precision of the grid is displayed in the lower right on the status bar.

In the Textured view, the camera controls are slightly more complicated because you are navigating in 3d space. The following table describes how the mouse buttons and motions affect the camera position in the Textured view: The columns of the table refer to the mouse motions and the rows refer to the mouse buttons.

The other way to activate Camera mode is to hold the Space bar down while clicking and moving the mouse. For example, to pan in an orthographic view, press and hold the Space bar while holding the left mouse button and moving in the view. When you stop the panning and let go of the space bar, you will be back in your original mode.
